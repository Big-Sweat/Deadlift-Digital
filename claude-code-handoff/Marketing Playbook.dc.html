WEBSITES THAT PULL WEIGHT · V1 · JULY 2026

1 — Positioning

What we are: a website studio for small businesses that need to look established and win customers online — without agency prices or DIY headaches.

One-liner: "We do the heavy lifting. You run your business." Every message reinforces the same promise: strength, reliability, no wasted motion.

Against the alternatives: vs. DIY builders (Wix/Squarespace) — done-for-you, done right; vs. big agencies — fixed price, fast turnaround, a human you can call; vs. freelancers — a real process, real support after launch.

2 — Brand identity

The mark: a 45-lb plate — the badge of doing the work. Reads at any size, from favicon to signage; the orange core is the single point of color.

  • Colors: Iron Black #17181A · Chalk #F6F4F0 · Plate Orange #E85D26 (accent only — CTAs and the plate core).
  • Type: Archivo Black for headlines and the wordmark; Space Grotesk for body; IBM Plex Mono for labels and small caps.
  • Voice: plainspoken, confident, gym-floor direct. Short sentences. No jargon. "We do the heavy lifting. You run your business."

3 — Target customers

  • Local service businesses (contractors, gyms, salons, clinics, restaurants) — need credibility + calls/bookings. Highest volume, fastest close.
  • New businesses launching — need everything: site, logo usage, Google presence. Highest urgency.
  • Established SMBs with outdated sites — embarrassed by their current site, losing to competitors. Highest ticket (redesign + ongoing care plan).

5 — Channels (in priority order)

  1. Referrals & partnerships — accountants, bookkeepers, sign shops, business coaches, chambers of commerce. Offer a referral fee. Cheapest, warmest leads for SMB web work.
  2. Google Business Profile + local SEO — rank for "web design [your city]". Your own site is the portfolio proof.
  3. Before/after content — LinkedIn + Instagram + TikTok: redesign reveals, "roast my website" teardowns, timelapses. The teardown format is the brand's signature.
  4. Outbound teardowns — send 10 free mini-teardown videos per week to local businesses with weak sites. Personal, useful, hard to ignore.
  5. Google Ads (later) — only after organic proof; target high-intent "website designer near me" queries.

6 — Funnel

SEE teardown content → GET free site teardown → BOOK 20-min call → BUY Starter/Heavy → STAY on Spotter plan → REFER (fee paid)

Every piece of content ends with the same CTA: "Get a free teardown of your website." One funnel, one CTA, repeated everywhere.

7 — Content pillars

  • Teardowns (40%) — "roast my website" critiques of real (anonymized or volunteered) small-business sites.
  • Before/after (30%) — client transformations with results (calls, bookings, speed scores).
  • Owner education (20%) — plain-English answers: "what should a website cost?", "do I need SEO?"
  • Shop floor (10%) — behind-the-scenes of builds; the "reps" culture of the studio.

8 — First 90 days

  • Days 1–30: roll out the plate-badge logo + own website; set up Google Business Profile; list 25 partner prospects; publish 3 teardowns.
  • Days 31–60: 10 outbound teardowns/week; sign 3 referral partners; land first 2–3 Starter clients (discounted for case-study rights).
  • Days 61–90: publish 2 before/after case studies; launch Spotter plan to all clients; review channel data and double down on the top performer.

9 — Metrics that matter

Teardowns sent per week · teardown → call rate · call → close rate · average project value · clients on Spotter plan · referral leads per month. Ignore vanity metrics (followers, impressions) for the first six months.
